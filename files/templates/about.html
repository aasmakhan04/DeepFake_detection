The Technology Behind DeepShield

DeepShield is an advanced computer vision project designed to tackle the growing issue of synthetic media and deepfakes. By leveraging Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), the system identifies subtle artifacts and inconsistencies left behind by AI image generators and editing software.

EfficientNet is highly optimized for speed while maintaining state-of-the-art accuracy through compound scaling. It uniformly scales network width, depth, and resolution for maximum performance.

ResNet (Residual Networks) utilizes skip connections or shortcuts to jump over some layers. This allows for significantly deeper networks without suffering from the vanishing gradient problem.

Xception relies on depthwise separable convolutions. It maps spatial correlations for each output channel separately, making it an incredibly efficient feature extractor.

Training Data

To ensure robust performance, the models were rigorously trained and evaluated using a carefully balanced subset derived from the comprehensive Celeb-DF v1 dataset, passing through a heavily augmented training pipeline.
